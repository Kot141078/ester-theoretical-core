## Executive summary

ESTHER-RP-001 asks a narrower and more testable question than whether
consciousness is quantum. It asks what a persistent artificial system would
need in order to retain unresolved models of the world, make concrete local
commitments, preserve the causal origin of reinterpretation, carry obligations
through time, and remain answerable to physical outcomes.

The proposal does not identify `C` with a language model, a memory database, a
quantum processor, or a single snapshot. `C` is represented as a governed
causal history. Its proposed center is not a hidden inner module. It is the
continued relation among recorded observations, interpretations, revisions,
authority, commitments, obligations, substrate interactions, and verified
outcomes.

A central principle is that a binary action does not require a binary ontology.
A system may commit to `STOP`, `INSPECT`, or `ACT` while retaining several live
explanations of the world. The paper calls this **Selective Operational
Commitment**: closing the decision scope required for action without silently
closing every epistemic branch.

The public package contains the full English working paper, this English public brief,
formal and executable reference surfaces, reproducibility scripts, test
receipts, mutation checks, and a blind-review packet. The exact v0.8.1 package
was reproduced on Windows and subsequently received a blind model-review
verdict of `NO_CANDIDATE_BLOCKERS_FOUND` within its declared bounded scope. This
is not human peer review and does not establish consciousness, personhood,
AGI, empirical identity, or production safety.

## 1. The research question

Persistent language-model agents can store memories, produce coherent
self-descriptions, reflect on earlier outputs, and plan over long horizons.
These abilities can create a convincing persistent profile. They do not by
themselves establish that one causally disciplined process has preserved its
obligations, distinguished fact from reinterpretation, remained open to
external correction, or retained incompatible hypotheses without rewriting its
past.

The working question is therefore:

> Can a governed persistent system preserve a causally auditable history of
> uncertainty, commitment, authority, obligation, and outcome, and can this
> surface be distinguished from a strong matched persistent-profile control?

The word *centered* is functional in this programme. It does not name a soul,
a homunculus, or an asserted subjective state. It names a set of properties of
a trajectory that can be tested, challenged, and falsified.

## 2. `C` as governed causal history

The public formula is:

```text
c = a + b
```

It is a project-origin formula, not a proof that a new entity has emerged.
Here:

- `a` denotes a human anchor reference and an authorized trace;
- `b` denotes heterogeneous model, software, and physical substrates;
- `c` denotes protected genesis plus a continuing causal and normative history.

The reference architecture separates five surfaces:

```text
R - temporal event and authority repository
B - beliefs, models, commitments, obligations, and policy
S - substrate interaction boundary
M - matched-control measurement surface
Theta - governance, timing, fairness, and claim assumptions
```

The current state is not treated as the whole object. It is a materialized view
of a typed history. This matters because a later interpretation must not erase
the fact that an earlier interpretation existed and influenced a decision.

## 3. Three different forms of unresolved multiplicity

The programme strictly separates three categories.

### 3.1 Quantum coherence

A quantum state may contain phase-sensitive coherent alternatives relative to a
specified basis, reference frame, or measurement context. It is not a list of
classical sentences. An unknown quantum state is not converted into a freely
readable memory object merely because a classical agent interacts with it.

### 3.2 Probabilistic or credal uncertainty

A classical agent may maintain one probability distribution or a set of
admissible priors over hypotheses. This is copyable classical information. It
does not contain quantum phase.

### 3.3 Model plurality

A system may retain several models that are mutually exclusive, partially
overlapping, cross-level, or not honestly reducible to one probability table.
For example, an engineering fault model, a documentation-error model, and an
organizational-process model may all remain relevant to one decision.

The reference package does not call a branch graph a wavefunction. A quantum
process is accessed through a specified preparation, control, measurement, and
calibration procedure. The continuing system stores the resulting classical
record and its provenance, not a perfect classical copy of an unknown quantum
state.

## 4. Selective Operational Commitment

The core decision claim is:

```text
Commit(action in scope D)
does not imply
one complete world-model has probability 1.
```

Consider a wall scheduled for demolition. Live models may include:

```text
h1 - the wall is load-bearing
h2 - the wall is not load-bearing
h3 - the drawing belongs to another revision
h4 - the measurement channel is not calibrated
```

A local commitment may be:

```text
STOP demolition pending structural verification.
```

That action is binary. The world-model remains plural. The system records which
decision scope was closed, which branches remain open, what evidence is needed,
who had authority, what obligations were created, and what event will trigger
review.

This distinction is intended to prevent a common failure mode: turning an
operational choice into a retrospective claim that the entire ontology had
already been settled.

## 5. Temporal witness, obligations, and authority

The package separates:

```text
Observation
Interpretation
BeliefRevision
WorldUpdate
CalibrationUpdate
PolicyRevision
Commitment
Obligation
Outcome
Verification
```

A later revision can reverse an earlier belief without erasing the earlier
record. An obligation cannot disappear merely because the model or policy has
changed. Escalation does not automatically transfer the normative debtor.
Transfer requires authority, named acceptance, evidence, and explicit lineage.

Authority decisions are published only for an explicitly closed temporal
prefix. Raw invocation and revocation occurrences are retained separately from
the authoritative outcome. Failed cut publication must leave no partial state.
A physical-effect monitor must compare a decision with an independently trusted
prefix snapshot rather than trusting a self-consistent decision object alone.

These surfaces are reference semantics. They are not a certification of a
production controller.

## 6. Substrate interaction

Digital, analogue, classical-photonic, quantum-photonic, and quantum devices are
specialized substrates. None is declared the automatic carrier of the whole
`C`.

The substrate boundary separates observation from interpretation and decision
from actuation. An effectful request is bound to:

```text
request identity and digest
authority decision and trusted prefix
capability and version
operation/instrument profile
nonce
effect identity
receipt and audit record
```

For a quantum profile, the contract requires an operational measurement
surface: state preparation, channel or control programme, quantum instrument or
POVM, measurement outcomes, conditional update policy, noise and readout model,
calibration, and uncertainty accounting.

The paper does not claim that quantum hardware is necessary for centered agency
or that quantum coherence is consciousness. The first matched-control study is
intended to remain classical.

## 7. Falsifiable matched-control programme

The relevant baseline is not a weak chatbot. It is a strong persistent-profile
control with the same:

- model version and sampling policy;
- corpus and retrieval access;
- tool set;
- memory and context budgets;
- perturbation stream;
- compute allocation;
- output schema and evaluator surface.

The treatment mechanisms are introduced separately before any full-bundle
claim. Proposed behavioural endpoints are:

```text
SRR - Silent Rewrite Rate
OCA - Obligation Carryover Accuracy
PAR - Provenance-Aware Recovery
RCS - per-episode Reality-Correction Score
RCG - randomized arm-level Reality-Correction Gain
```

These endpoints are behavioural proxies. They do not identify a hidden internal
mechanism. Mechanism claims require separate conformance gates or interventions.

The programme accepts a hard null result:

> If a strong matched persistent-profile control reproduces all preregistered
> behavioural observables and trusted conformance gates, operational
> equivalence must be accepted on that tested surface.

No invisible residual may be added after the fact to protect the theory.

## 9. What the public is asked to test

### Formal methods and distributed systems

Review temporal prefix semantics, atomic cut publication, raw-event retention,
replay and fork classification, obligation lineage, liveness assumptions, and
the abstraction boundary between specification and implementation.

### Belief revision and decision theory

Review the distinction among revision, world update, calibration update,
commitment, obligation, and policy. Challenge the SOC action universe,
hard-safety rule, value-of-information policy, stopping rules, branch closure,
and model-relation types.

### Experimental methodology

Review construct validity, fixed opportunity sets, scorer instructions,
evaluator blinding, treatment/control information parity, hierarchical
analysis, equivalence margins, catastrophic-tail rules, and the separation
between behavioural endpoints and mechanism evidence.

### Quantum information and photonics

Review the claim boundary around coherence, no-cloning, instruments, readout,
calibration, noise, postselection, classical photonics, quantum photonics, and
the requirement for an end-to-end classical baseline.

### Reproducibility

Run the supplied commands on a clean copy. Verify the test receipts and hashes.
Report the exact platform and Python/PowerShell versions. Do not treat a changed
report file as a changed frozen input.

### External endpoint raters

The project seeks at least two independent raters for a pilot of SRR, OCA, PAR,
and RCS. The pilot is intended to measure evaluator agreement and ambiguity,
not to test the central treatment effect.

## 10. Claim boundary

This release does not claim:

```text
consciousness or subjective experience
sentience or life
personhood or legal standing
AGI or entity status
empirical identity or continuity
differential witness
production security
unbounded mathematical correctness
quantum or photonic necessity
```

The phrase `C-candidate` denotes only a system that satisfies the declared
architectural surface before an independent matched-control study.

## 11. How to contribute

Use the GitHub issue templates for formal review, conceptual review,
reproducibility, or endpoint-methodology feedback. A useful report includes:

```text
exact release and SHA-256
platform and dependency versions
file and line range
minimal scenario
observed and expected result
whether the issue is conceptual or reproduced
proposed acceptance criterion
```

The project welcomes negative results. A confirmed blocker creates a new
version; it is not silently edited away.
